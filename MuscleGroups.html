Muscle Groups

Starting with the bro split...
The bro split means working each muscle in its individual session. An example of the bro split would be:

Monday Tuesday Wednesday Thursday Friday
Back Chest Shoulders Biceps Triceps

As you can see, the bro split only allows you to work each muscle once per week. Although this split means you have longer per session to work with higher volume, two times per week per muscle is the optimum way to train.


Push Pull Legs

My favourite way to train
The Push Pull Legs (PPL) split means working your full upper body twice per week.

Push

During your Push days, you are training each muscle that performs the 'Push' motion. This includes exercises like the Bench Press, Shoulder Press and Tricep Pushdown.

Push = Chest, Shoulders and Triceps

Pull

During Pull days, any exercices that perform a 'Pull' motion will be worked. This includes exercises such as the Lat Pulldown or Bicep Curl.

Pull = Back and Biceps

Legs

Self explanatory... Legs!

You can split this up in any way you like. Legs Push Pull, Push Pull Legs, Push Legs Pull etc etc...
The main thing in a Push Pull Legs routine is that you run through the three routines over and over, regardless of order, providing you don't do the same muscle group on consecutive days. More about that here.


Full Upper Body

Another one of my favourites...

The Full Upper means working your full upper body in each session. I would only recommend this if you have a day inbetween each session or combined with the Push Pull Legs. If i can only get three sessions per week I like to combine 2 days in a row with a Push Pull followed by a rest day, finished with a Full Upper.


Full Body

I rarely do Full Body sessions. They're great if you can only workout 2-3 times per week but thats not very often for me.

In a Full Body session... you guessed it! You work every muscle per session.
